## Block 1 — British Shadowing (20 min)

**Source:** BBC 6 Minute English "environment climate" — search this on YouTube or at bbc.co.uk/learningenglish

**Week 1 Phonology Focus: Non-rhotic vowels + final consonant clusters**
Specifically drill:
- Non-rhotic review — all four vowels: /ɑː/ (car), /ɪə/ (here), /ɛə/ (there), /ɔː/ (more) — say each with no r
- Final cluster drill sequence: "plants" → /plɑːnts/, "emissions" → /ɪmɪʃənz/, "effects" → /ɪfekts/ — articulate every consonant
- Linking across word boundaries: "climate_action" — the t in "climate" links to the vowel opening "action": /klaɪmɪtækʃən/

**Protocol:**
1. Listen once, no transcript (2 min)
2. Listen again with transcript (2 min)
3. Shadow at 0.75x speed on YouTube (5 min)
4. Shadow at full speed (5 min)
5. Record 30 seconds of yourself. Play back. Note: run through all four non-rhotic vowels — are all four correct? Are your final clusters intact when followed by silence? (6 min)

## Block 2 — Speaking: Task 1 Independent (30 min)

**Template:** templates/speaking_templates.md → Task 1

**Prompt:** "Should cities ban private cars?"

**Steps:**
1. Read the Task 1 template (2 min)
2. Prep: 15 seconds — write your position + 2 reasons in shorthand
3. Record response (45 seconds) — use the template opening: "In my opinion…" or "I strongly believe…"
4. Play back. Score yourself:
   - Delivery (1-4): ___
   - Language (1-4): ___
   - Development (1-4): ___
5. Identify ONE fix (e.g., position stated too late, second reason underdeveloped, pace)
6. Record again applying that fix

## Block 3 — Reading: Academic Skimming (25 min)

**Topic:** Science/technology

Search "TOEFL reading practice science technology passage" for a 700-word practice passage.

**Skimming protocol:**
1. Read title only → predict: what is this about? (30 sec)
2. Read ONLY the first sentence of each paragraph (2 min)
3. Write: thesis = ___, main arguments = (1) ___ (2) ___ (3) ___
4. Answer the questions WITHOUT re-reading (15 min)
5. Review wrong answers: A=vocab B=inference C=main idea D=detail E=rhetorical purpose (5 min)
