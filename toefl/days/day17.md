## Block 1 — British Shadowing (20 min)

**Source:** English with Lucy "British RP connected speech" — search "English with Lucy connected speech British RP" on YouTube

**Week 2 Phonology Focus: Sentence stress + weak forms**
Specifically drill:
- Elision (sounds disappear): "next door" → /neks dɔː/ (the t drops), "must be" → /məs biː/ (t drops), "last night" → /lɑːs naɪt/
- Assimilation (sounds change): "that person" → /ðæp pɜːsən/ (t becomes p before bilabial), "good morning" → /ɡʊm mɔːnɪŋ/
- Weak form "have" (auxiliary): "I have been" → /aɪ əv bɪn/, "they have gone" → /ðeɪ əv ɡɒn/ — only strong when main verb

**Protocol:**
1. Listen once, no transcript (2 min)
2. Listen again with transcript (2 min)
3. Shadow at 0.75x speed on YouTube (5 min)
4. Shadow at full speed (5 min)
5. Record 30 seconds of yourself. Play back. Note: are you dropping the t in "next" and "must" in connected speech, or are you still pronouncing every sound separately? (6 min)

## Block 2 — Speaking: Task 2 Campus Announcement (30 min)

**Template:** templates/speaking_templates.md → Task 2

**Prompt:** Student housing policy — the university is changing the off-campus housing application process, requiring students to apply through a new online portal six months in advance (NoteFull style). Read the announcement for 30 seconds, then listen to two students discuss it for 1 minute, then respond.

**Steps:**
1. Read the Task 2 template (2 min)
2. Prep: 30 seconds — note the change + student's opinion (agree/disagree) + the reasons they give from the conversation
3. Record response (60 seconds) — use the template opening: "According to the announcement…"
4. Play back. Score yourself:
   - Delivery (1-4): ___
   - Language (1-4): ___
   - Development (1-4): ___
5. Identify ONE fix (e.g., student reaction not clearly stated, announcements details missing, rushed ending)
6. Record again applying that fix

## Block 3 — Reading: Academic Skimming (25 min)

**Topic:** Environment

Search "TOEFL reading practice environment conservation ecology passage" for a 700-word practice passage.

**Skimming protocol:**
1. Read title only → predict: what is this about? (30 sec)
2. Read ONLY the first sentence of each paragraph (2 min)
3. Write: thesis = ___, main arguments = (1) ___ (2) ___ (3) ___
4. Answer the questions WITHOUT re-reading (15 min)
5. Review wrong answers: A=vocab B=inference C=main idea D=detail E=rhetorical purpose (5 min)
