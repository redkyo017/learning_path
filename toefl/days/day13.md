## Block 1 — British Shadowing (20 min)

**Source:** BBC 6 Minute English "language learning" — search this on YouTube or at bbc.co.uk/learningenglish

**Week 2 Phonology Focus: Sentence stress + weak forms**
Specifically drill:
- Content words carry stress, function words are weak: "I WANT to LEARN aBOUT lanGUAGE" — stress WANT, LEARN, BOUT, GUAGE
- Weak form "to": "want to" → /wɒntə/, "going to" → /ɡɒnə/, "have to" → /hæftə/
- Weak form "and": "reading and writing" → "reading ən writing" — the /d/ in "and" often disappears

**Protocol:**
1. Listen once, no transcript (2 min)
2. Listen again with transcript (2 min)
3. Shadow at 0.75x speed on YouTube (5 min)
4. Shadow at full speed (5 min)
5. Record 30 seconds of yourself. Play back. Note: are your content words clearly stressed and your function words reduced? Does "want to" sound like /wɒntə/ or is it still fully pronounced? (6 min)

## Block 2 — Speaking: Task 2 Campus Announcement (30 min)

**Template:** templates/speaking_templates.md → Task 2

**Prompt:** University policy change — the university is requiring all first-year students to live on campus for their first semester (NoteFull style). Read the announcement for 30 seconds, then listen to two students discuss it for 1 minute, then respond.

**Steps:**
1. Read the Task 2 template (2 min)
2. Prep: 30 seconds — note the change + student's opinion (agree/disagree) + one reason they give
3. Record response (60 seconds) — use the template opening: "According to the announcement…"
4. Play back. Score yourself:
   - Delivery (1-4): ___
   - Language (1-4): ___
   - Development (1-4): ___
5. Identify ONE fix (e.g., student's reason not clearly stated, transition between announcement and opinion unclear)
6. Record again applying that fix

## Block 3 — Reading: Academic Skimming (25 min)

**Topic:** Psychology

Search "TOEFL reading practice psychology cognition passage" for a 700-word practice passage.

**Skimming protocol:**
1. Read title only → predict: what is this about? (30 sec)
2. Read ONLY the first sentence of each paragraph (2 min)
3. Write: thesis = ___, main arguments = (1) ___ (2) ___ (3) ___
4. Answer the questions WITHOUT re-reading (15 min)
5. Review wrong answers: A=vocab B=inference C=main idea D=detail E=rhetorical purpose (5 min)
