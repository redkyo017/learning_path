## Block 1 — British Shadowing (15 min) — NON-NEGOTIABLE

**Source:** BBC 6 Minute English — search "BBC 6 Minute English" and choose any recent episode

**Week 5 Phonology Focus: Connected Speech — Linking, Elision, and Assimilation Combined**

1. Linking review: pick two phrases from the episode where consonant-to-vowel linking occurs. Shadow each 5 times, focusing on the smooth join between words.
2. Elision in clusters: find a phrase with /t/ or /d/ deletion in consonant clusters (e.g., "facts and figures" → "fac(ts) and figures"). Shadow with and without the deletion, then adopt the natural reduced form.
3. Rhythm and weak forms: identify function words (a, the, to, of, and) and confirm the speakers use weak forms /ə/, /tə/, /əv/, /ən/. Shadow with matching weak forms.

**Protocol:**
1. Listen once (2 min)
2. Shadow at full speed (8 min)
3. Record 30 seconds. Check that linking, elision, and weak forms are all present in your output (5 min)

## Block 2 — Full Writing Section (60 min)

**Task 1 (Integrated, 20 min):**
Search "TOEFL integrated writing practice astronomy passage" — choose any practice set.
Use templates/writing_templates.md → Task 1 template. Write 200-225 words.
Self-score:
- Coverage: Did you address all 3 lecture points? (Yes/Partial/No)
- Language: Did you vary reporting verbs? (Yes/No)
- Organisation: Clear intro → 3 body paragraphs → conclusion? (Yes/No)

**Task 2 (Academic Discussion, 10 min):**
Prompt: "Should companies be legally required to offer flexible working hours?"
Use templates/writing_templates.md → Task 2. Write 120-150 words.
Self-score:
- Position stated clearly in sentence 1? (Yes/No)
- Two supported reasons given? (Yes/No)
- Engaged with one classmate's point? (Yes/No)

**Review (30 min):**
Read both responses aloud. Count word length for each.
Check: did you use at least 4 different AWL collocations across both responses?
List any collocations you want to use more often.
