## Block 1 — British Shadowing (15 min) — NON-NEGOTIABLE

**Source:** BBC 6 Minute English — search "BBC 6 Minute English" and choose any recent episode

**Week 6 Phonology Focus: Intonation Polish — Information Structure and Emphasis**

1. Given vs. new information: in connected speech, "new" information receives stronger stress than "given" information. Find an exchange or explanation in the episode. Shadow with stronger emphasis on new information and reduced emphasis on information already established.
2. List intonation: find a list of 3 or more items in the episode. Shadow with a rising tone on each item except the last, which takes a falling tone.
3. Parenthetical de-accentuation: find a phrase inserted parenthetically (e.g., "..., as you know, ..."). Shadow with noticeably lower pitch on the parenthetical phrase.

**Protocol:**
1. Listen once (2 min)
2. Shadow at full speed (8 min)
3. Record 30 seconds. Check that your emphasis placement distinguishes new from given information (5 min)

## Block 2 — Full Writing Section (60 min)

**Task 1 (Integrated, 20 min):**
Search "TOEFL integrated writing practice environmental science passage" — choose any practice set.
Use templates/writing_templates.md → Task 1 template. Write 200-225 words.
Self-score:
- Coverage: Did you address all 3 lecture points? (Yes/Partial/No)
- Language: Did you vary reporting verbs? (Yes/No)
- Organisation: Clear intro → 3 body paragraphs → conclusion? (Yes/No)

**Task 2 (Academic Discussion, 10 min):**
Prompt: "Is it better to specialise in one field or develop broad knowledge across many fields?"
Use templates/writing_templates.md → Task 2. Write 120-150 words.
Self-score:
- Position stated clearly in sentence 1? (Yes/No)
- Two supported reasons given? (Yes/No)
- Engaged with one classmate's point? (Yes/No)

**Review (30 min):**
Read both responses aloud. Count word length for each.
Check: did you use at least 4 different AWL collocations across both responses?
List any collocations you want to use more often.
