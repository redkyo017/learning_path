## Block 1 — British Shadowing (20 min)

**Source:** BBC Radio 4 "In Our Time science" — search "BBC Radio 4 In Our Time science episode" on YouTube or at bbc.co.uk/programmes/b006qykl

**Week 1 Phonology Focus: Non-rhotic vowels + final consonant clusters**
Specifically drill:
- Academic register non-rhotic: "theory" → /θɪərɪ/, "era" → /ɪərə/, "period" → /pɪərɪəd/ — glide smoothly
- Schwa reduction: "the" → /ðə/ before consonants (not /ðiː/), "of" → /əv/, "from" → /frəm/
- Final clusters in academic speech: "concepts" → /kɒnsepts/, "experiments" → /ɪkˈsperɪmənts/, "developments" → /dɪˈveləpmənts/

**Protocol:**
1. Listen once, no transcript (2 min)
2. Listen again with transcript (2 min)
3. Shadow at 0.75x speed on YouTube (5 min)
4. Shadow at full speed (5 min)
5. Record 30 seconds of yourself. Play back. Note: Week 1 final consolidation — check all three features: non-rhotic vowels, final clusters, schwa reductions. (6 min)

## Block 3 — Reading: Academic Skimming (25 min)

**Topic:** Reading mock weekend — use this block for a short TOEFL-style reading passage.

Search "TOEFL reading practice astronomy or geology passage" for a 700-word practice passage on a natural science topic different from biology.

**Skimming protocol:**
1. Read title only → predict: what is this about? (30 sec)
2. Read ONLY the first sentence of each paragraph (2 min)
3. Write: thesis = ___, main arguments = (1) ___ (2) ___ (3) ___
4. Answer the questions WITHOUT re-reading (15 min)
5. Review wrong answers: A=vocab B=inference C=main idea D=detail E=rhetorical purpose (5 min)
