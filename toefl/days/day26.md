## Block 1 — British Shadowing (15 min) — NON-NEGOTIABLE

**Source:** Cambridge University YouTube — search "Cambridge lecture humanities"

**Week 5 Phonology Focus: Connected Speech — Full Review of Linking, Elision, Assimilation**

1. Linking: find two consonant-to-vowel links. Shadow each phrase 3 times.
2. Elision: find one instance of /t/ deletion in a cluster. Shadow the natural reduced form.
3. Assimilation: find one cross-word assimilation (e.g., "in particular" → "im particular"). Shadow until it sounds effortless.

**Protocol (condensed for Phase 3):**
1. Listen once (2 min)
2. Shadow at full speed — no slow speed in Phase 3 (8 min)
3. Record 30 seconds. Check all three connected-speech features are present in your delivery (5 min)

## Weekend Block A — Writing Section (60 min)

**Task 1 (Integrated, 20 min):**
Search "TOEFL integrated writing practice psychology passage" — choose any practice set.
Use templates/writing_templates.md → Task 1 template. Write 200-225 words.
Self-score:
- Coverage: Did you address all 3 lecture points? (Yes/Partial/No)
- Language: Did you vary reporting verbs? (Yes/No)
- Organisation: Clear intro → 3 body paragraphs → conclusion? (Yes/No)

**Task 2 (Academic Discussion, 10 min):**
Prompt: "Is social media more beneficial or harmful to society?"
Use templates/writing_templates.md → Task 2. Write 120-150 words.
Self-score:
- Position stated clearly in sentence 1? (Yes/No)
- Two supported reasons given? (Yes/No)
- Engaged with one classmate's point? (Yes/No)

**Review (30 min):** Read both responses aloud, count words, check AWL collocation density.
