## Block 1 — British Shadowing (20 min)

**Source:** BBC 6 Minute English "technology robots" — search this on YouTube or at bbc.co.uk/learningenglish

**Week 2 Phonology Focus: Sentence stress + weak forms**
Specifically drill:
- Nuclear stress (the most important word in a phrase): "Robots will REPLACE some jobs" vs "ROBOTS will replace some jobs" — meaning shifts with stress
- Weak form "for": "designed for" → /dɪˈzaɪnd fə/, "used for" → /juːzd fə/ — /fɔː/ only when "for" is at the end of a sentence
- Weak form "at": "look at this" → /lʊk ət ðɪs/, "good at" → /ɡʊd ət/ (not /ɡʊd æt/)

**Protocol:**
1. Listen once, no transcript (2 min)
2. Listen again with transcript (2 min)
3. Shadow at 0.75x speed on YouTube (5 min)
4. Shadow at full speed (5 min)
5. Record 30 seconds of yourself. Play back. Note: pick one sentence from your recording and identify the nuclear stress — is it on the most important word? Are "for" and "at" reduced? (6 min)

6. Record again applying that fix

## Block 3 — Reading: Academic Skimming (25 min)

**Topic:** Technology/innovation

Search "TOEFL reading practice technology innovation passage" for a 700-word practice passage.

**Skimming protocol:**
1. Read title only → predict: what is this about? (30 sec)
2. Read ONLY the first sentence of each paragraph (2 min)
3. Write: thesis = ___, main arguments = (1) ___ (2) ___ (3) ___
4. Answer the questions WITHOUT re-reading (15 min)
5. Review wrong answers: A=vocab B=inference C=main idea D=detail E=rhetorical purpose (5 min)
