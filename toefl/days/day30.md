## Block 1 — British Shadowing (15 min) — NON-NEGOTIABLE

**Source:** BBC Radio 4 In Our Time — search "In Our Time philosophy episode" and pick any philosophy episode

**Week 6 Phonology Focus: Intonation Polish — Emphasis Placement**

1. Nuclear stress drill: in RP, one syllable per intonation unit carries the highest pitch — the nucleus. Find a sentence and move the nucleus to different words, changing meaning each time (e.g., "She DIDN'T leave early" vs. "She didn't LEAVE early"). Shadow all variants.
2. Contrastive stress: find a sentence in the episode where the speaker emphasises a word to contrast with something previously mentioned (e.g., "not the first experiment, the SECOND one"). Shadow with the same contrastive stress.
3. De-accenting: find a sentence where the speaker does NOT stress a word you might expect to stress because it was already mentioned (anaphoric de-accentuation). Shadow with that reduced prominence.

**Protocol (condensed for Phase 3):**
1. Listen once (2 min)
2. Shadow at full speed — no slow speed in Phase 3 (8 min)
3. Record 30 seconds. Check that emphasis placement matches the speaker's intent — not every content word is equally stressed (5 min)
