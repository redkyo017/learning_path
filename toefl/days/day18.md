## Block 1 — British Shadowing (20 min)

**Source:** Cambridge University YouTube "chemistry" — search "Cambridge University chemistry lecture" on YouTube

**Week 2 Phonology Focus: Sentence stress + weak forms**
Specifically drill:
- Technical vocabulary stress: "CHEMistry", "rEACtion", "moLEcule", "comPOUND" — stress falls on different syllables; drill each carefully
- Weak forms in lecture style: "which is" → /wɪtʃ ɪz/, "that is" → /ðət ɪz/, "there is" → /ðər ɪz/ (linking r before vowel is acceptable in RP)
- Rhythm in listing: "first… second… and third…" — "first" and "second" are fully stressed; "and" is always reduced to /ən/

**Protocol:**
1. Listen once, no transcript (2 min)
2. Listen again with transcript (2 min)
3. Shadow at 0.75x speed on YouTube (5 min)
4. Shadow at full speed (5 min)
5. Record 30 seconds of yourself. Play back. Note: are technical words stressed on the correct syllable? Is "and" always reduced to /ən/ in your lists? (6 min)

## Block 3 — Reading: Academic Skimming (25 min)

**Topic:** Writing tasks weekend — use this block to read about academic discussion writing strategies.

Search "TOEFL academic discussion writing practice environmental policy passage" for a reading passage that sets up the integrated writing task.

**Skimming protocol:**
1. Read title only → predict: what is this about? (30 sec)
2. Read ONLY the first sentence of each paragraph (2 min)
3. Write: thesis = ___, main arguments = (1) ___ (2) ___ (3) ___
4. Answer the questions WITHOUT re-reading (15 min)
5. Review wrong answers: A=vocab B=inference C=main idea D=detail E=rhetorical purpose (5 min)
