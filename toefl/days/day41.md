# Day 41 — Speaking Fluency + Writing Precision

**Phase:** 4 — Simulation | **Time:** 2 hours | **Magoosh: review only**

---

## Block 1 — Shadowing (15 min)

**Source:** BBC Radio 4 In Our Time — any recent episode.
Shadow one 5-minute segment at full speed.
Focus on the intonation pattern that still feels least natural to you.

---

## Block 2 — Speaking: Template Fluency (45 min)

The goal today is automaticity — the template should feel effortless.

**Round 1 (15 min):** Do all 4 task type openings from memory without looking at the template.
- Task 1: "I believe that ___ for two main reasons..."
- Task 2: "The announcement states that ___ . The man/woman [agrees/disagrees]..."
- Task 3: "According to the reading, ___ refers to ___ . The professor illustrates..."
- Task 4: "In the lecture, the professor discusses ___ by providing ___ examples..."
Record each opening. British RP, falling intonation on statements.

**Round 2 (30 min):** Do 2 full Speaking tasks back-to-back, timed.
Choose Task 2 and Task 4 (your integrated tasks — the ones most learners underperform).
Source: search "TST Prep TOEFL Speaking Task 2 practice" and "TST Prep TOEFL Speaking Task 4 practice".
Self-score each. Log in progress_tracker.md.

---

## Block 3 — Writing: Precision Review (30 min)

Retrieve your Writing responses from Mock 2 (Day 39).
Read Task 1 response aloud. Ask:
- Did every paragraph address ONE lecture point clearly?
- Did you use at least 3 different reporting verbs?

Read Task 2 response aloud. Ask:
- Is your position stated in the first sentence?
- Does every sentence connect to your stated position?

Rewrite any paragraph that fails these checks.

---

## Block 4 — AWL Collocation Final Sprint (10 min)

Pick 5 AWL collocations you have never used in a production sentence.
Write one sentence for each. Say them aloud.
