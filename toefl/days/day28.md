## Block 1 — British Shadowing (15 min) — NON-NEGOTIABLE

**Source:** Cambridge University YouTube — search "Cambridge lecture economics"

**Week 6 Phonology Focus: Intonation Polish — Statement vs. Question Intonation**

1. Falling intonation on declaratives: pick two statements from the lecture. Shadow with a clear falling tone on the last stressed syllable. Record and confirm the fall is audible.
2. Rising intonation on yes/no questions: if the lecture contains any questions, shadow those. If not, construct 2 yes/no questions using vocabulary from the lecture and say them with rising tone.
3. Contrast drill: take one sentence and say it as (a) a statement with falling tone, then (b) a yes/no question with rising tone. Record both. Confirm the difference is unmistakable.

**Protocol (condensed for Phase 3):**
1. Listen once (2 min)
2. Shadow at full speed — no slow speed in Phase 3 (8 min)
3. Record 30 seconds. Check that statements end with a clear fall and that any questions end with the appropriate contour (5 min)

**Task 3 (Academic concept):**
Read: 45 sec | Listen: ~90 sec | Prep: 30 sec | Response: 60 sec

**After all 4 tasks:** Log scores in progress_tracker.md.
