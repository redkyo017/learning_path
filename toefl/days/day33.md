## Block 1 — British Shadowing (15 min) — NON-NEGOTIABLE

**Source:** Cambridge University YouTube — search "Cambridge lecture social science"

**Week 6 Phonology Focus: Intonation Polish — Emphasis and Discourse Organisation**

1. Topic shift signalling: find a moment where the speaker moves to a new topic (e.g., "Now, turning to...", "Another key point is..."). Shadow with the characteristic high pitch reset that marks a new discourse unit.
2. Contrastive emphasis: find a contrast in the lecture (e.g., "not X, but Y"). Shadow with strong stress on both contrasted items and a slight pause between them.
3. Rhetorical question intonation: find or construct a rhetorical question. In academic British RP, these often have a falling-rising contour. Shadow and record, verifying the fall-rise is audible.

**Protocol (condensed for Phase 3):**
1. Listen once (2 min)
2. Shadow at full speed — no slow speed in Phase 3 (8 min)
3. Record 30 seconds. Check that topic shifts, contrasts, and rhetorical questions each have a distinct intonation shape (5 min)
