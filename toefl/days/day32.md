## Block 1 — British Shadowing (15 min) — NON-NEGOTIABLE

**Source:** Cambridge University YouTube — search "Cambridge lecture neuroscience" or "Cambridge lecture psychology"

**Week 6 Phonology Focus: Intonation Polish — Emphasis in Academic Argumentation**

1. Thesis statement emphasis: academics typically emphasise their main claim with a high pitch onset on the key noun or verb. Find a claim statement in the lecture. Shadow with heavy stress on the key word.
2. Hedging intonation: find a hedged statement (e.g., "it may be argued that...", "this suggests that..."). The hedge word often carries reduced stress. Shadow with that reduced prominence on "may", "might", "appears to".
3. Conclusion signalling: find a concluding or summarising phrase (e.g., "In summary...", "This demonstrates..."). Shadow with the falling tone that signals finality.

**Protocol (condensed for Phase 3):**
1. Listen once (2 min)
2. Shadow at full speed — no slow speed in Phase 3 (8 min)
3. Record 30 seconds. Check that your academic intonation signals argument structure — claims, hedges, and conclusions all sound distinct (5 min)
