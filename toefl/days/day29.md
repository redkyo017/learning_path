## Block 1 — British Shadowing (15 min) — NON-NEGOTIABLE

**Source:** English with Lucy — search "English with Lucy British intonation"

**Week 6 Phonology Focus: Intonation Polish — Rising vs. Falling on Questions**

1. Wh-question intonation: find or construct 3 wh-questions (who, what, where, why, how). These take falling intonation in RP. Shadow with a clear fall from the question word downward.
2. Yes/no question intonation: find or construct 3 yes/no questions. These take rising intonation in RP. Shadow with a clear rise at the end.
3. Tag question drill: find a tag question (e.g., "That's interesting, isn't it?"). Shadow the tag: falling tag = certainty, rising tag = genuine question. Record and compare.

**Protocol:**
1. Listen once (2 min)
2. Shadow at full speed (8 min)
3. Record 30 seconds. Check that your wh-questions fall and your yes/no questions rise — do NOT let both types end with a rise (a common L1 interference pattern) (5 min)

## Block 2 — Full Writing Section (60 min)

**Task 1 (Integrated, 20 min):**
Search "TOEFL integrated writing practice biology ecology passage" — choose any practice set.
Use templates/writing_templates.md → Task 1 template. Write 200-225 words.
Self-score:
- Coverage: Did you address all 3 lecture points? (Yes/Partial/No)
- Language: Did you vary reporting verbs? (Yes/No)
- Organisation: Clear intro → 3 body paragraphs → conclusion? (Yes/No)

**Task 2 (Academic Discussion, 10 min):**
Prompt: "Should governments invest more in space exploration or solving problems on Earth?"
Use templates/writing_templates.md → Task 2. Write 120-150 words.
Self-score:
- Position stated clearly in sentence 1? (Yes/No)
- Two supported reasons given? (Yes/No)
- Engaged with one classmate's point? (Yes/No)

**Review (30 min):**
Read both responses aloud. Count word length for each.
Check: did you use at least 4 different AWL collocations across both responses?
List any collocations you want to use more often.
