## Block 1 — British Shadowing (15 min) — NON-NEGOTIABLE

**Source:** Cambridge University YouTube — search "Cambridge University lecture" and pick any lecture that interests you

**Week 6 Phonology Focus: Intonation Polish — Consolidation of All Intonation Patterns**

1. Full intonation inventory: in one minute of the lecture, find and label: one statement (falling), one yes/no question (rising), one wh-question (falling), and one emphasis placement. If any type is absent, construct your own example using vocabulary from the lecture.
2. Discourse chunking: pick a complex sentence with a subordinate clause. The main clause should carry the nuclear stress; the subordinate clause should be lower in pitch. Shadow with that hierarchy.
3. L1 interference final check: record yourself shadowing 30 seconds. Listen specifically for any residual Vietnamese-origin intonation patterns — flat tone on unstressed syllables, level pitch on clause-final position, rising tone on all statements. Mark any remaining issues for Phase 4 focus.

**Protocol (condensed for Phase 3):**
1. Listen once (2 min)
2. Shadow at full speed — no slow speed in Phase 3 (8 min)
3. Record 30 seconds. Check all intonation patterns — statements, questions, emphasis, and clause hierarchy (5 min)
