## Block 1 — British Shadowing (15 min) — NON-NEGOTIABLE

**Source:** English with Lucy — search "English with Lucy advanced British pronunciation"

**Week 5 Phonology Focus: Connected Speech — Elision**

1. Elision drill: find phrases where sounds are dropped in fast speech (e.g., "next day" → "nex' day", "last night" → "las' night"). Shadow until the omission feels natural, not sloppy.
2. /t/ elision between consonants: find words ending in /t/ before another consonant (e.g., "exactly", "perfectly"). Shadow the reduced forms.
3. Schwa reduction: identify unstressed syllables reduced to /ə/ (e.g., "pronunciation" → /prə.ˌnʌn.si.ˈeɪ.ʃən/). Mark them in your notes and shadow with reduction.

**Protocol:**
1. Listen once (2 min)
2. Shadow at full speed (8 min)
3. Record 30 seconds. Check elision — are you omitting the right sounds without distorting meaning? (5 min)

## Block 2 — Full Writing Section (60 min)

**Task 1 (Integrated, 20 min):**
Search "TOEFL integrated writing practice archaeology passage" — choose any practice set.
Use templates/writing_templates.md → Task 1 template. Write 200-225 words.
Self-score:
- Coverage: Did you address all 3 lecture points? (Yes/Partial/No)
- Language: Did you vary reporting verbs? (Yes/No)
- Organisation: Clear intro → 3 body paragraphs → conclusion? (Yes/No)

**Task 2 (Academic Discussion, 10 min):**
Prompt: "Students should be required to study abroad for at least one semester. Do you agree?"
Use templates/writing_templates.md → Task 2. Write 120-150 words.
Self-score:
- Position stated clearly in sentence 1? (Yes/No)
- Two supported reasons given? (Yes/No)
- Engaged with one classmate's point? (Yes/No)

**Review (30 min):**
Read both responses aloud. Count word length for each.
Check: did you use at least 4 different AWL collocations across both responses?
List any collocations you want to use more often.
