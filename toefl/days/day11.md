## Block 1 — British Shadowing (20 min)

**Source:** Cambridge University YouTube "biology" — search "Cambridge University biology lecture" on YouTube

**Week 1 Phonology Focus: Non-rhotic vowels + final consonant clusters**
Specifically drill:
- Academic non-rhotic vocabulary: "research" → /rɪˈsɜːtʃ/ (RP — no final r), "culture" → /kʌltʃə/, "structure" → /strʌktʃə/
- Scientific final clusters: "cells" → /selz/, "results" → /rɪzʌlts/, "organisms" → /ˈɔːɡənɪzəmz/
- Connected non-rhotic: "water evaporates" — "water" ends in schwa /ˈwɔːtə/, not /ˈwɔːtər/

**Protocol:**
1. Listen once, no transcript (2 min)
2. Listen again with transcript (2 min)
3. Shadow at 0.75x speed on YouTube (5 min)
4. Shadow at full speed (5 min)
5. Record 30 seconds of yourself. Play back. Note: are academic words like "research" and "structure" fully non-rhotic, and are your scientific cluster words clean? (6 min)

## Block 3 — Reading: Academic Skimming (25 min)

**Topic:** Writing tasks weekend — use this block to read about integrated writing strategies.

Search "TOEFL integrated writing practice reading passage" for a 230-word reading passage with a lecture counterargument.

**Skimming protocol:**
1. Read title only → predict: what is this about? (30 sec)
2. Read ONLY the first sentence of each paragraph (2 min)
3. Write: thesis = ___, main arguments = (1) ___ (2) ___ (3) ___
4. Answer the questions WITHOUT re-reading (15 min)
5. Review wrong answers: A=vocab B=inference C=main idea D=detail E=rhetorical purpose (5 min)
