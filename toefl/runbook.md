# TOEFL Daily Runbook

Your operational guide for running each day of the 45-day sprint.
Open this when you are unsure what to do next, or when you need to troubleshoot.

---

## 1. Daily Startup (2 min — do this before anything else)

1. Open `days/dayNN.md` for today's number
2. Glance at `progress_tracker.md` — check your Magoosh streak is intact
3. Set a physical timer (phone timer, not a tab) for your total session time
4. Sit at a desk. Headphones in. Phone face-down or in another room.

That is it. Do not check email or Slack first. Start Block 1 immediately.

---

## 2. The Session Formula (Weekday — 2 hours)

Run the blocks in order. Do not reorder them.

| Block | Time | What you do |
|-------|------|-------------|
| 1 Shadowing | 20 min | Shadow a British speaker. Record 30 sec. Check phonology. |
| 2 Speaking | 30 min | Record a TOEFL task. Play back. Fix one thing. Record again. |
| 3 Reading | 25 min | Skim one passage. Answer questions. Categorise wrong answers. |
| 4 Listening | 15 min | Listen. Write 3 bullet notes. Check against transcript. |
| 5 Vocabulary | 20 min | 20 Magoosh words. One production sentence each. Say them aloud. |
| 6 Transfer | 10 min | Rewrite one work email using today's vocabulary. |

**If you run out of time:** never cut Block 1 (Shadowing) or Block 2 (Speaking). Cut from Block 3 or 4 first — your stronger sections can absorb a short session. Never cut Block 5 (vocabulary production sentences deactivate if skipped).

---

## 3. The Shadowing Protocol (Block 1)

Most learners shadow incorrectly — they listen and mouth words at the same time. That is not shadowing. Follow this exact sequence:

**Phase 2 (Days 06-20) — full protocol:**
1. Listen once, no transcript, no repeating. Just listen for rhythm and melody. (2 min)
2. Listen again with the transcript open. Read along. Notice words that surprised you. (2 min)
3. Play at 0.75x speed on YouTube (Settings → Playback speed). Shadow aloud — your voice overlapping the speaker's. (5 min)
4. Play at 1.0x speed. Shadow aloud. (5 min)
5. Stop the video. Record yourself speaking 30 seconds of the same content from memory or re-shadow a section. Play it back. (6 min)

**Phase 3 (Days 21-35) and Phase 4 (Days 36-43) — condensed:**
- Skip the 0.75x step. Shadow at full speed only.
- Record 30 seconds. Check one specific phonology target. Done.

**What to check when you play back your recording:**

| Week | Check this specifically |
|------|------------------------|
| 1-2 | Final consonants: did you say "facts" or "fac"? "desks" or "des"? |
| 1-2 | Non-rhotic 'r': "better" → /ˈbɛtə/ not /ˈbɛtər/ |
| 3-4 | Sentence stress: are you stressing CONTENT words and reducing function words? |
| 3-4 | Weak forms: "want to" → /wɒnə/, "going to" → /ɡənə/ |
| 5-6 | Linking: does "an_apple" sound like one word? |
| 7 | Intonation: do statements fall at the end (not rise like questions)? |

**Troubleshooting shadowing:**
- *"I can't keep up at full speed."* → Stay at 0.75x for one more week. Do not force full speed until you can stay with the speaker.
- *"I sound robotic."* → You are over-focusing on individual words. Shadow phrases, not words. Focus on the rhythm of the whole sentence.
- *"I don't hear a difference between my recording and the original."* → You are not listening critically. Compare one sentence at a time. Use YouGlish.com to hear the target word in native speech, then compare directly.

## 4. The Speaking Protocol (Block 2)

**Setup:**
- Use your phone's default voice memo app or any free recorder
- Speak toward the wall (not into the microphone directly — projects your voice and slows pace)
- Keep a physical timer running for prep and response times

**The cycle — do this every session:**
1. Read the prompt (or listen for integrated tasks)
2. Set timer for prep time (15 sec Task 1 / 30 sec Tasks 2-3 / 20 sec Task 4)
3. Write 3 bullet points only during prep — not full sentences
4. Record your response (45 sec Task 1 / 60 sec Tasks 2-4)
5. Play back immediately. Score yourself:
   - Delivery (1-4): pace, clarity, intonation
   - Language (1-4): vocabulary range, sentence variety
   - Development (1-4): clear position, specific examples, template structure
6. Identify ONE specific thing to improve (not "speak better" — "my second reason had no example")
7. Record again applying only that fix
8. Log your scores in `progress_tracker.md`

**Self-scoring guide:**
- Score 4: sounds like a competent non-native speaker on a good day
- Score 3: understandable, mostly structured, minor gaps
- Score 2: understandable but choppy, vague, or under-time
- Score 1: hard to follow, major pauses, no structure

**Minimum acceptable session:** 2 full recording cycles (original + one fix). More is better but two is the floor.

**Integrated task note-taking (Tasks 2, 3, 4):**
Draw this table before the listening starts — do not wait:
```
Reading point / Lecture response
-----------------------------------
Point 1:        |
Point 2:        |
Point 3 (T3/T4):|
```
Fill it during the audio. Speak from the table, not from memory.

## 5. The Vocabulary Protocol (Block 5)

**The wrong way:** open Magoosh, tap through 20 cards, mark each as "know it" or "don't know it", close app. This creates passive recognition only — the word will not come to you under test pressure.

**The right way:**

For each of the 20 new words:
1. Read Magoosh's definition and example sentence (15 sec)
2. Close the card — do not look at it
3. Write one sentence of your own using that word in `vocabulary/production_notebook.md` (30 sec)
4. The sentence must describe something real — your work, your life, something you know
5. Say the sentence aloud once with British RP intonation

Total: ~1 minute per word = 20 minutes for 20 words.

**Weekly AWL collocations (from `vocabulary/awl_collocations.md`):**
- Study 10 new collocations per week (not per day — weekly batch)
- Each collocation: write one sentence, say it aloud
- Revisit previous weeks' collocations by using them in your Speaking Block or Transfer Block

**The transfer exercise (Block 6):** this is where vocabulary becomes permanent. When you use a word from today's Magoosh session in a real work context (your actual email, your actual report), it wires to a real memory. Do not skip this block. It takes 10 minutes and it is the highest-ROI 10 minutes of the session.

## 6. Weekend Rhythm (4 hours)

Weekends are your deep work days. The structure in your day file is correct — follow it. A few additional notes:

**Writing blocks (60 min):**
- Task 1 (Integrated, 20 min): Set a strict timer. Stop at 20 min even if unfinished. Real test is 20 min.
- Task 2 (Academic Discussion, 10 min): Set a strict timer. Stop at 10 min. Real test is 10 min.
- Review (30 min): Read both responses aloud. Count words. Check for AWL collocations. Rewrite any paragraph that has no specific example.

**Extended shadowing (30 min):**
The weekend session should use a longer, denser source than weekday BBC 6 Minute English. Cambridge University lectures and BBC Radio 4 In Our Time are longer and denser — they build stamina. Follow the condensed protocol (no slow-speed step on weekends in Phase 3+).

**Full section mocks:**
- These are timed. Do not pause them.
- After completing: score yourself, identify top 2 error categories, log in `progress_tracker.md`.
- Do not spend more than 20 minutes reviewing errors — identify the category, log it, move on. Deep drilling happens between mock tests, not immediately after.

---

## 7. Weekly Check-In (Sunday evening, 10 min)

At the end of each Sunday, answer these 4 questions in `progress_tracker.md`:

1. **Magoosh streak:** did you hit 20 new words every session this week? If not, which day(s) did you skip?
2. **Speaking trend:** are your self-scores going up, flat, or down compared to last week?
3. **Phonology check:** which specific L1 pattern improved this week? Which is still stubborn?
4. **AWL usage:** did you use this week's collocations in at least one Speaking response AND one Transfer exercise?

If any answer is "no" or "down": the next week's first session should address that specifically before moving to new content.

---

## 8. If You Miss a Day

**Miss 1 day:** resume the next day on the scheduled day file. Do not try to catch up by doubling up. Doubling up creates fatigue and poor retention. Accept the missed day and continue.

**Miss 2 consecutive days:** on your return, do a 30-minute "re-entry session" first: 10 min shadowing + 10 min Magoosh review cards only (no new words) + 10 min Speaking (one task). Then resume your scheduled day file the following day.

**Miss 3+ days:** reset to a slightly earlier phase. If you were on Day 22, resume at Day 20. The overlap is a feature — it rebuilds momentum without backtracking far.

**Never skip Block 1 (Shadowing):** phonology resets faster than any other skill when practice stops. If you can only do 15 minutes today, do 15 minutes of shadowing. Everything else is secondary.

---

## 9. Low Energy Day Minimum

When you are tired, stressed, or short on time — do these 3 things only:

1. **Shadowing: 15 min** (mandatory — never skip)
2. **Magoosh: 20 new words + production sentences** (10 min)
3. **One Speaking recording** (Task 1, no playback needed) (5 min)

Total: 30 minutes. This keeps your phonology and vocabulary active on hard days. It is not ideal — but it is vastly better than skipping entirely.

## 10. Phase Transition Checklist

Before moving from one phase to the next, check all boxes:

### Phase 1 → Phase 2 (after Day 05)
- [ ] Read all 4 ETS scoring rubrics
- [ ] Watched at least 2 scored Speaking samples on TST Prep
- [ ] Took a full diagnostic test and scored it
- [ ] Built your personal error category map in `progress_tracker.md`
- [ ] Completed first Magoosh session (20 words + production sentences)
- [ ] Completed first shadowing session

### Phase 2 → Phase 3 (after Day 20)
- [ ] Magoosh streak: 15+ consecutive days
- [ ] Speaking: self-score averages 2+ on all dimensions
- [ ] Template openings for all 4 Speaking tasks memorised (can say them without reading)
- [ ] Mock section scores (from Day 11 or 18): Reading 22+, Listening 22+
- [ ] Week 1 and Week 2 AWL collocations have each been used in at least one production sentence

### Phase 3 → Phase 4 (after Day 35)
- [ ] Full Speaking section mock score estimated at 20+
- [ ] Full Writing section: covering all 3 Task 1 points consistently
- [ ] Magoosh streak: unbroken since Day 05 (or near-unbroken with makeup days)
- [ ] Connected speech drills show improvement from Phase 2 recordings
- [ ] Phase 3 Sprint Review (Day 35) completed and written in notebook

## 11. Mock Test Protocol (Days 36, 39, 42)

These three mock tests determine your final test-day readiness. Run each one as if it is real.

**Before the test:**
- Clear your desk
- Use headphones
- Set phone to silent, face-down or in another room
- Have blank paper only — no notes, no vocabulary lists

**During the test:**
- Do not pause the recording
- Do not look up answers
- Do not check the time more than once per section

**After the test:**
1. Score immediately while the experience is fresh
2. Enter all scores in `progress_tracker.md`
3. Write your weakest section in one sentence: "Today my weakest section was ___ because ___."
4. That section is your ONLY focus until the next mock. Everything else maintains.

**Between mocks — targeted repair only:**
Do not try to improve everything between mocks. The gap between Mock 1 (Day 36) and Mock 2 (Day 39) is 2 days. Pick ONE subsection weakness and drill it specifically. Spreading attention across all four sections in 2 days produces no measurable improvement.

## 13. Test Day Checklist

Print or screenshot this the day before the test:

- [ ] ID confirmed (passport or national ID)
- [ ] Test centre address confirmed, travel time checked
- [ ] Arrive 30 minutes early
- [ ] Eat a normal breakfast — nothing heavy, nothing new
- [ ] No reviewing vocabulary or notes in the morning
- [ ] Headphones (if you bring your own — check test centre rules)
- [ ] Confirmation number saved

**In the test room:**
- Speak toward the back of the room (projects voice, slows pace)
- Start every Speaking response with the template opening — do not improvise
- Task 1 timings: 15 sec prep / 45 sec response
- Tasks 2-4 timings: 30 sec prep (20 sec Task 4) / 60 sec response
- Writing Task 1: cover all 3 lecture counterpoints
- Writing Task 2: position in sentence 1, 120+ words minimum
