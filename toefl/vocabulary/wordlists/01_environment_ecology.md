# Vocabulary: Environment & Ecology

**Word count:** 386
**Study method:** Read 20 words per session. For each word: read the definition, cover it, say the definition from memory, then say the example sentence aloud using British RP.

**ecosystem** *(noun)* — /EE-koh-sis-tum/
Definition: A community of living organisms interacting with each other and with their non-living physical environment as a functional unit.
Example: "The destruction of the coastal ecosystem has led to the collapse of local fish populations."
Collocations: aquatic ecosystem | terrestrial ecosystem | ecosystem collapse

**ecological** *(adjective)* — /ee-kuh-LAH-jih-kul/
Definition: Relating to ecology or the relationships between living organisms and their environment.
Example: "Researchers conducted an ecological survey of the region before approving the mining project."
Collocations: ecological balance | ecological impact | ecological restoration

**biodiversity** *(noun)* — /by-oh-dih-VER-sih-tee/

Example: "The Amazon Basin contains the highest levels of biodiversity of any region on the planet."
Collocations: biodiversity loss | biodiversity hotspot | biodiversity conservation

**habitat** *(noun)* — /HAB-ih-tat/

Example: "Urban expansion has dramatically reduced the natural habitat of the red-tailed hawk."
Collocations: habitat destruction | habitat loss | critical habitat

**species** *(noun)* — /SPEE-sheez/
Definition: A group of organisms that share common characteristics and can interbreed to produce fertile offspring.
Example: "Scientists estimate that thousands of species become extinct every year before they are even discovered."
Collocations: threatened species | invasive species | species richness

**subspecies** *(noun)* — /SUB-spee-sheez/
Definition: A taxonomic rank below species, referring to a distinct population within a species that differs in some characteristics.
Example: "The Amur leopard is a critically endangered subspecies found only in the Russian Far East."
Collocations: distinct subspecies | recognized subspecies | subspecies differentiation

**predator** *(noun)* — /PRED-uh-ter/
Definition: An animal that hunts, kills, and eats other animals for food.
Example: "The reintroduction of wolves as apex predators dramatically altered the behaviour of deer populations."
Collocations: apex predator | natural predator | predator-prey relationship

**prey** *(noun/verb)* — /pray/

Collocations: easy prey | prey population | prey on smaller animals

**carnivore** *(noun)* — /KAR-nih-vor/

**herbivore** *(noun)* — /HER-bih-vor/

**omnivore** *(noun)* — /AHM-nih-vor/

**scavenger** *(noun)* — /SKAV-in-jer/

Example: "Vultures are highly efficient scavengers that prevent the spread of disease by consuming carrion."
Collocations: obligate scavenger | carrion scavenger | scavenger species

**decomposer** *(noun)* — /dee-kum-POH-zer/

**producer** *(noun)* — /pruh-DOO-ser/

**consumer** *(noun)* — /kun-SOO-mer/

**temperate** *(adjective)* — /TEM-per-it/
