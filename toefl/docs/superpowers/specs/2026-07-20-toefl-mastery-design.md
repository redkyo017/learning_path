# TOEFL iBT Mastery Plan — 45-Day Aggressive Sprint

**Date:** 2026-07-20
**Status:** Approved design

---

## Purpose

Reach a TOEFL iBT score of 90-100+ in 45 days for a UK master's program application,
while simultaneously improving daily professional English fluency. The plan is anchored
on Approach C: test forensics first, output-led interleaving, with Speaking as the
primary section trained every day from Day 1.

---

## Learner Context

- **Native language:** Vietnamese
- **Current level:** Professional working English (daily use), not academic
- **TOEFL experience:** None — first attempt
- **Weakest section:** Speaking
- **Accent goal:** British RP (Received Pronunciation)
- **Target score:** 90-100+ (floor 92, aim 100) for UK master's program
- **Time budget:** 2 hours/weekday, 4 hours/weekend (~115 hours total over 45 days)
- **Existing tools:** Magoosh flashcard app
- **Secondary goal:** Improve daily working English (emails, meetings, reports)

| Section   | Duration     | Score | Tasks                                        |
|-----------|-------------|-------|----------------------------------------------|
| Reading   | 54-72 min   | 0-30  | 3-4 passages, 10 questions each              |
| Listening | 41-57 min   | 0-30  | 3-4 lectures, 2-3 conversations              |

## Five Core Strategy Principles

### 1. Learn the Rubric Before Learning the Content
TOEFL raters use a specific scoring grid. Study ETS scoring descriptors for all 4 sections
before touching a single practice question. Know exactly what earns 24+ vs 18 in Speaking:
delivery, language use, and topic development — not just "speaking well."

### 2. Output Drives Vocabulary
Every new word encountered must be used in a Speaking or Writing output within 24 hours.
Magoosh flashcards are the retrieval trigger, not the primary learning event. The cycle:
see word → write a sentence → say it aloud → use it in a work email rewrite.
This wires the word to production channels simultaneously.

### 3. Speaking Trained Every Day from Day 1
Vietnamese L1 habits to eliminate with daily drilling:
- Final consonant dropping ("desks" → "des", "texts" → "tex")
- Syllable-timed rhythm (every syllable given equal weight — English is stress-timed)
- Rising intonation on declarative statements
These take 30-40 days of daily practice to rewire — exactly the available window.

### 4. British RP via Shadowing, Not Rules
Accent is acquired through motor memory, not IPA charts. Daily 15-20 minute shadowing
of selected British academic speakers rewires pronunciation faster than any explicit study.
Shadowing protocol: listen once → repeat at 70% speed → repeat at full speed → record and compare.

### 5. Professional English as Daily Transfer
End each session with 5-10 minutes rewriting one real work communication (email, Slack
message, or report paragraph) using vocabulary and structures from that day's session.
This bridges TOEFL skills directly to working English.

## Mistakes That Waste 80% of Beginners' Time

1. **Studying vocabulary in isolation.** Passive flashcard review without production creates
   recognition that vanishes under test pressure. Every word must be used, not just seen.

2. **Taking full mock tests before building technique.** Repeating a broken approach 15 times
   does not fix it. Technique comes first; mocks are measurement tools, not learning tools.

3. **Reading TOEFL passages like novels.** Academic skimming — identify thesis, topic sentences,
   and conclusion structure — is a trainable skill. Reading every word is both slower and less accurate.

4. **Saving Speaking for last.** Phonological habits from Vietnamese take weeks to rewire.
   Starting on Day 30 guarantees you run out of time.

5. **Ignoring integrated tasks.** Speaking Tasks 2-4 and Writing Task 1 require summarizing
   listening content accurately. Most beginners drill only independent tasks.

6. **Not studying the scoring rubric.** The rubric is public. Top scorers reverse-engineer it.
   Most beginners never read it.

7. **Targeting general English improvement instead of test conventions.** TOEFL rewards specific
   academic discourse conventions. A "good" general English response can score poorly if it
   lacks the expected structure.

## Vocabulary System (Target: ~3000 words)

### Composition
| Source                                   | Count  | Method                                              |
|------------------------------------------|--------|-----------------------------------------------------|
| Magoosh TOEFL flashcards (new + review)  | ~1200  | 20 new/day × 30 active days = 600 new introduced; remaining 600 encountered via spaced review |
| Academic Word List (AWL)                 | ~400   | Collocations, not isolated words                    |
| Activated passive vocabulary             | ~1400  | Production exercises                                |

AWL source: search "Academic Word List Victoria University Wellington" — available free as a PDF and Anki deck.

### Daily Vocabulary Cycle
1. **Morning:** Review 20 new Magoosh words (5 min)
2. **During session:** Write one production sentence per new word in a notebook
3. **Speaking slot:** Say 3-5 sentences aloud using that day's words (British shadowing cadence)
4. **Evening:** Use 2-3 new words in the daily work email rewrite
5. **Magoosh spaced review:** Let the app schedule reviews — do not skip review sessions

### Collocation Over Definition
Do not learn: "mitigate = reduce"
Learn instead: "mitigate the impact of X", "mitigate risks associated with X"
Academic words travel in fixed collocations — learning those patterns is 3x faster than
learning definitions and guessing usage.

## 45-Day Phase Structure

### Phase 1 — Test Forensics & System Setup (Days 1-5)
**Daily time:** 2 hours weekdays

**Goal:** Understand the game before playing it. Set up all systems.

| Day | Focus |
|-----|-------|
| 1   | Read ETS scoring rubrics for all 4 sections (ETS.org). Watch 2 scored Speaking samples on TST Prep. Note: what exactly earns 24+ vs 18? |
| 2   | Watch 2 scored Writing samples (TST Prep/NoteFull). Study Speaking task types 1-4. Configure Magoosh: 20 new words/day. |
| 3   | Take one full timed TOEFL practice test (ETS free sample). Do not study results yet — simulate test conditions only. |
| 4   | Score analysis. Map every error to a category: vocab gap / pronunciation / structure / time / strategy. |
| 5   | Setup production notebook. First 20 Magoosh words. First 10-min British shadowing session (BBC 6 Minute English). |

---

### Phase 2 — Foundation Sprint (Days 6-20)
**Daily time:** 2 hours weekdays, 4 hours weekends

**Goal:** Build all four skill foundations simultaneously. Fix core Vietnamese L1 phonology habits.

**Weekday daily structure (2 hours):**
| Block       | Duration | Activity |
|-------------|----------|----------|
| Shadowing   | 20 min   | BBC 6 Minute English or English with Lucy — shadow 3× per segment |
| Speaking    | 30 min   | 2 TOEFL Speaking tasks using TST Prep templates — record, replay, fix one thing |
| Reading     | 25 min   | One TOEFL passage with academic skimming technique (thesis → topic sentences → conclusion) |
| Listening   | 15 min   | ELLLO or BBC 6 Minute English with transcript — transcribe 2 minutes without looking |
| Vocab       | 20 min   | 20 new Magoosh words → write one production sentence each |
| Transfer    | 10 min   | Rewrite one work email/message using today's vocabulary |

**Weekend additional blocks (4 hours total):**
- 60 min: Writing (1 integrated task + 1 academic discussion task, timed)
- 30 min: Full section mock (Reading or Listening, alternating weekends)
- 30 min: Shadow one 10-min British academic lecture at half speed then full speed
- Extra vocab/AWL collocation review

**British RP focus targets (Weeks 1-2):**
- Week 1: Non-rhotic vowels ("car" = "cah", "here" = "hia") + final consonant clusters
- Week 2: Sentence stress and weak forms ("I want to go" → "I wanna go" in connected speech)

---

### Phase 3 — Integrated Practice Sprint (Days 21-35)
**Daily time:** 2 hours weekdays, 4 hours weekends

**Goal:** Shift from skill drills to full section simulations. Internalize templates.

**Weekday rotation:**
| Day       | Primary Focus |
|-----------|--------------|
| Mon/Wed/Fri | Full Speaking section (all 4 tasks timed) — self-score with rubric |
| Tue/Thu   | Full Writing section (both tasks timed) — self-score with rubric |
| Daily     | 15 min shadowing (non-negotiable) + 40 Magoosh cards (20 new + 20 review) |

**Weekends:**
- Full Reading mock section (timed) — Saturday
- Full Listening mock section (timed) — Sunday
- 60 min: Shadow one Cambridge University lecture at half speed then full speed
- AWL collocation review: 30 min

**Integrated task drill (critical — most beginners skip this):**
- Speaking Tasks 2-4 require listening to a passage then speaking. Practice note-taking
  while listening: capture key argument, 2 supporting details, speaker's stance.
- Writing Task 1 requires reading + listening + synthesizing. Drill the 3-step structure:
  point from reading → how lecture addresses it → your synthesis sentence.

---

### Phase 4 — Full Simulation & Targeting (Days 36-43)
**Daily time:** 2 hours weekdays, 4 hours weekends

**Goal:** Simulate real test conditions. Eliminate remaining weak points.

- Every 3 days: Full 3-hour timed TOEFL mock (ETS Practice Online)
- Between mocks: Target only your lowest subscore from the previous mock
- Daily 15-min shadow slot maintained — phonology is still consolidating
- Vocab: Shift from new words to collocation review and AWL patterns
- Speaking: Focus on delivery score — pacing, pause placement, stress patterns

---

### Days 44-45 — Rest & Mental Prep
- No new content
- Light review of your personal Speaking response templates (10 min max)
- Confirm test-day logistics: location, ID, arrival time
- Sleep 8 hours both nights

## Professional English Integration

The following transfer exercises run daily throughout all phases. They take 5-10 minutes
and directly improve working English alongside TOEFL preparation:

1. **Email rewrite:** Take one real work email you sent and rewrite it using today's
   academic vocabulary and more precise structure.
2. **Meeting prep:** Before a work meeting, prepare 2-3 sentences using TOEFL-level
   language to express your position or question. Use them in the meeting.
3. **Report elevation:** Once per week, take one paragraph from a work report and apply
   TOEFL Writing Task 2 structure (claim → evidence → analysis → conclusion).

## Success Criteria

- **Day 5:** Baseline diagnostic scored. Personal error category map created.
- **Day 20:** Speaking responses consistently use TST Prep template structure. Final consonant
  dropping noticeably reduced. Magoosh streak maintained.
- **Day 35:** Mock section scores: Reading 24+, Listening 24+, Speaking 20+, Writing 22+
- **Day 43:** Full mock score 90+. Speaking score 22+.
- **Day 45:** Rested. Templates memorized. Test-day ready.

## Anti-Patterns to Watch For

- Skipping the daily 15-min shadow slot ("I'll double up tomorrow") — phonology resets without daily input
- Doing Magoosh passively without production sentences — creates recognition only, not recall
- Spending more than 25% of time on Reading/Listening (your stronger sections)
- Avoiding recording your own Speaking responses — discomfort = the signal you need to practice more
- Switching resources mid-plan — pick the listed resources and stay with them
